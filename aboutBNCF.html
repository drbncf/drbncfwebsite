Dr. B. N. Choubey Foundation (BNCF) was formed in the memory of Dr. Barmeshwar Nath Choubey (1934-1985).

ABOUT DR. BARMESHWAR NATH CHOUBEY

Dr. Barmeshwar Nath Choubey was born into a farmer's family in Buxar, Bihar, India. He has servaed in the government of the state of Bihar. He was a great social activist in the post independence era, and actively worked towards the upliftment of the poor and downtrodden people and farmers. He promoted the implementation of scientific methods of crop cultivation most prominently in the erstwhile state of Shahabad (Bihar) which has now been bifurcated into four districts i.e., Bhojpur, Buxar, Rohtas, Kaimur. His work has always been appreciated by the local people as well as the local administration. He was a great protagonist of the Panchayati Raj System and believed in the idea of devolution of power.

"If one female is educated, the entire family becomes more inclined towards education"

-Dr. Barmeshwar Nath Choubey

He used to organise open classes and lectures for the poor and the illiterate and tried to find solutions to their problems related to education, farming, health etc. He was a strong advocate of education of women in India, especially in rural India. He believed that if one female is educatied, the entire family will be more inclined towards education. He propagated the idea that "Knowledge is Power" and this power can be used at any time, anywhere.

Dr. Choubey was greatly concerned with the welfare of people from backward and scheduled caste without ignoring the poor from the upper caste as well. He always emphasized the true meaning of the freedom struggle, and the freedom we achieved, that there should be education, employment, good health and equality in the society.
